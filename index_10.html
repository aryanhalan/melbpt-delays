01 — Overview

How each mode performs against its government target

Transport Victoria sets minimum punctuality targets for each operator. A service is counted as "on time" if it arrives within 4 minutes and 59 seconds of its scheduled time. The yellow tick marks on each chart show where the target sits — green bars are meeting it, red bars are not.

Key finding: V/Line regional trains are the only mode consistently missing their 92% punctuality target, sitting 8.3 percentage points below threshold in April 2026. Interestingly, all modes meet their reliability targets — trains are mostly running, they're just running late.
Punctuality by Mode — April 2026
% of services arriving on time · yellow tick = government target
Each bar shows one mode's on-time rate. V/Line (red bar) is far below its 92% target. Metro Train, Tram, and Bus are all above theirs.
Reliability by Mode — April 2026
% of scheduled services actually run · yellow tick = target
Reliability measures whether services ran at all — separate from whether they were on time. All modes are meeting their reliability targets despite punctuality shortfalls.
02 — Monthly Trends

12 months of punctuality — patterns and a worrying decline

Tracking performance from May 2025 to April 2026 reveals consistent seasonal dips in summer (December) and a sharp recent decline in V/Line. Click any mode in the legend of the main chart to highlight it.

Monthly Punctuality — All Modes (May 2025 – April 2026)
Click a mode in the legend to highlight it · hover for exact values
All four modes dip in December 2025, reflecting summer heat delays and reduced timetables over the holiday period. V/Line's April 2026 reading of 83.7% is its worst in the entire 12-month window — a troubling recent drop.
Metro Train — Monthly
Stays in a tight band around 94%
Metro trains are the most consistent mode — fluctuating within just 2.5 percentage points over the year.
Metro Tram — Monthly
Road congestion drives variation
Tram punctuality is more variable than trains, as trams share road space with other traffic and are affected by congestion.
V/Line — Monthly
Never reaches its 92% target
V/Line missed its 92% punctuality target every single month in this 12-month period. The April 2026 figure is the lowest point.
03 — Metro Train Lines

Not all lines are equal — outer suburbs suffer most

Metro Trains Melbourne operates 16 lines. Lines extending to outer suburbs consistently underperform inner-city routes, likely due to longer distances, shared freight track, and fewer redundancy options when things go wrong.

Peak Punctuality by Train Line
Sorted worst to best · colour shows punctuality level (red = poor, green = good) · hover for details
Stony Point (85.2%) and Mernda (86.5%) are the two worst-performing lines. Both extend well beyond 35km from the CBD. Sandringham (94.1%) and Williamstown (93.8%) top the list — both are shorter, inner-suburban routes.
Annual Cancellations by Line — 2025
Total cancelled services, sorted highest to lowest
Stony Point has the most cancellations (723) largely because it runs diesel single-car trains and lacks the redundancy of the electrified metro network. Mernda, Hurstbridge, and Pakenham — all outer lines — each exceed 450 cancellations.
Peak vs Off-Peak Punctuality by Line
Every line performs better during off-peak hours
Each dot represents one train line. All points fall above the diagonal, confirming that every line runs more on time during off-peak hours than at peak. The gap averages 4.7 percentage points across all lines.
04 — Geographic Pattern

The further from the city, the worse the delays

Rather than a traditional map, this chart plots each line's terminus distance from the CBD against its peak punctuality. The pattern is clear: lines that extend further from the city tend to be less punctual and record more cancellations.

Train Line Performance vs Distance from CBD
Each bubble = one train line · bubble size = annual cancellations · colour = punctuality (red poor → green good) · hover for details
There is a clear negative trend: as distance increases, punctuality falls and cancellations rise. Stony Point (62km, diesel operation) is the extreme outlier. Sandringham and Williamstown — short inner-city routes — sit at the top-left, with high punctuality and few cancellations.
05 — Deeper Analysis

Where things are getting better or worse

A heatmap of monthly target achievement and a year-on-year comparison show that V/Line is getting significantly worse, while Metro Bus is the only mode showing genuine improvement.

Target Achievement Heatmap — 12 Months
Green = above target · Red = below target · hover for exact value
V/Line is entirely red across all 12 months — it never hit its target once. Metro Tram dipped below target in December 2025. Metro Train, Bus remained consistently above their targets.
Year-on-Year Change (Apr 2025 vs Apr 2026)
Change in punctuality in percentage points
V/Line has dropped 4.7 percentage points year-on-year — the steepest fall of any mode. Metro Bus is the only mode showing improvement (+0.9pp), quietly outperforming all others.
Average Delay per Late Service by Train Line
Minutes late per delayed service · colour-coded by line group · hover for details
When a service is late, how late is it? Stony Point passengers face an average of 5.8 minutes per delayed service — nearly 3× the Sandringham experience (2.1 min). Outer lines not only have more delays, but those delays are also longer.

About this visualisation

This page analyses punctuality and reliability across Melbourne's public transport network — metro trains, trams, V/Line regional trains, and metropolitan buses — from May 2025 to April 2026.
